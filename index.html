NCLEX Preparation at Abrahams

Cracking NCLEX: Knowledge Before Strategy

At Abrahams, NCLEX preparation is an extension of professional nursing education, not as a standalone test-taking exercise. The NCLEX is designed to assess how a nurse thinks in real clinical situations, specifically how priorities are set, risks are identified, and patient safety is protected.

Next Generation NCLEX (NGN)

The Next Generation NCLEX places strong emphasis on clinical judgment and reasoning. Questions are no longer limited to single correct answers; instead, they reflect real-world scenarios where nurses must assess, analyse, prioritise, and evaluate care.

Recognised Question Banks

Practice plays a critical role. Recognised NCLEX question banks such as UWorld, Kaplan, and Archer Review are incorporated thoughtfully into the learning process.

Global Nursing Pathways

Nursing is regulated differently across countries. This section presents a factual, country-wise overview of nursing registration systems commonly pursued by internationally educated nurses.

United States of America

A Structured, Exam-Centred Licensure System

  • Educational Credential Evaluation (CGFNS, etc.)
  • English Proficiency (IELTS/TOEFL)
  • State Board Application & ATT
  • NCLEX-RN Examination (Mandatory)

United Kingdom

A Competency-Based Route Without NCLEX

  • NMC Application & Credential Review
  • English Proficiency (IELTS/OET)
  • Test of Competence: CBT (Computer Based Test) + OSCE (Clinical Exam)
  • NCLEX is NOT used.

Australia

Blending Global Standards

  • Regulated by AHPRA
  • NCLEX may be accepted for theoretical knowledge assessment in certain pathways.
  • Requires English Proficiency (IELTS/OET).

New Zealand

Competence-Focused

Regulated by NCNZ. NCLEX is not mandatory. Pathway involves verification and often a Competence Assessment Programme (CAP).

Canada

National Exam / Provincial Reg

Requires NNAS assessment. NCLEX-RN is the national licensure exam for most provinces.

Empowering Nurses & Elevating Careers

Changing the Perspective

The scope of practice of a Registered Nurse (RN) in developed countries differs significantly from that in India. In many Western healthcare systems, nurses are recognized as highly competent professionals who share a substantial portion of clinical responsibilities traditionally associated with physicians. A well-developed RN is capable of conducting comprehensive patient histories, performing physical assessments, identifying patient needs, planning care, implementing interventions, and evaluating outcomes independently.

However, in several parts of the world, the role of the RN remains underestimatedâ€”not only by the public but also by healthcare professionals themselves. Nurses are often viewed primarily as individuals who carry out medical orders rather than as autonomous clinical decision-makers.

Abrahams aims to change this perspective. By emphasizing advanced clinical assessment skills, sound clinical judgment, patient education at admission and discharge, and evidence-based nursing practice, we equip nurses with the knowledge and confidence required for expanded professional roles.
